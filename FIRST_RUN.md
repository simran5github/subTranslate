# Getting Started - First Run Guide

Welcome to SubTranslate! Here's how to get it working in the next 5 minutes.

## Step 1: Load the Extension (2 minutes)

### In Brave Browser:

1. **Open Extensions Page**
   - Click the menu (⋮) → Extensions → Manage Extensions
   - OR type `brave://extensions/` in address bar

2. **Enable Developer Mode**
   - Toggle switch in top-right corner → ON

3. **Load Extension**
   - Click blue **"Load unpacked"** button
   - Navigate to the `subTranslate` folder
   - Click **"Select Folder"** or **"Open"**

**That's it!** You should see SubTranslate in your extension list.

## Step 2: Find the Extension Icon (1 minute)

1. Look in your **toolbar** (top-right area of Brave)
2. Find the **movie camera icon** (🎬) - that's SubTranslate
3. If you don't see it:
   - Click the **puzzle piece icon** (extensions menu)
   - Look for SubTranslate in the list
   - Click the **pin icon** to add it to toolbar

## Step 3: Configure Settings (1 minute)

1. Click the SubTranslate icon (🎬)
2. A popup opens with settings

**Basic Setup:**
- ✅ **Enable Translation** - Toggle this ON
- ✅ **Source Language** - Leave as "English"
- ✅ **Target Language** - Select "French" (or your language)
- ✅ **Translation Provider** - Keep "LibreTranslate"

3. Click somewhere else to close popup

**Done!** Settings are saved automatically.

## Step 4: Test It Works (1 minute)

1. Go to **YouTube.com**
2. Find ANY video with subtitles
3. Click the **CC icon** in video player to enable subtitles
4. Watch the subtitles appear in your target language!

**✨ It works!**

## Troubleshooting First Run

### I don't see the extension icon
- Try clicking the puzzle piece (extensions menu) in toolbar
- Is Developer mode enabled? (It should show a colored background)

### Subtitles aren't translating
- Is the toggle **ON** in the popup?
- Do the video subtitles appear in original language first?
- Try refreshing the page (Ctrl+R)

### "API Error" message
- Your internet connection may be down
- LibreTranslate server may be busy
- Try a different provider: Google Translate or MyMemory

### Nothing happened
- Try a different website (Netflix, Disney+, etc.)
- Make sure subtitles are enabled (CC button in video)
- Check your browser console for errors (F12 → Console)
